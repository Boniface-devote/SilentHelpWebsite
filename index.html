The problem

Emergency systems still assume you can talk.

Most emergency workflows in Uganda run on voice calls — dispatchers who listen, responders who ask questions out loud, contacts who expect a ring. For Deaf and hard-of-hearing people, that design choice can mean the difference between help arriving and help never being reached.

01

Deaf users often can't call emergency services or hearing contacts quickly when something goes wrong.

02

Responders and contacts frequently don't know the caller is Deaf, which slows down or derails the response.

03

Local sign language learning and everyday phrase tools are scarce, making day-to-day communication harder too.

The solution

SilentHelp replaces the phone call with a tap, a location, and a message — then keeps a record so every SOS attempt stays visible and accountable.

  • 1
    One-tap SOS, no dial tone Opens the phone's own SMS app with an emergency message and location already filled in.
  • 2
    Safety checks before it's needed SOS stays locked until an emergency contact is on file, so help always has somewhere to go.
  • 3
    A record you can trust Every attempt is logged locally with a timestamp and status, from prepared to sent.
  • 4
    Communication beyond the emergency Quick phrases and sign language lessons support daily conversations, not just crises.
What the app does

Six tools, one bottom tab away.

Every screen is built around the same rule: information should be seen and felt, never just heard.

Emergency SOS

A large red button prepares a pre-filled SMS with location, backed by strong haptic feedback and a toast confirmation.

Profile & emergency contact

A completion checklist shows exactly which safety details are missing before SOS is unlocked.

SOS history log

Every attempt is stored with date, time, and status — prepared, opened in SMS, or cleared.

Multi-language support

The full app is available in English, Swahili, and Luganda, switchable from Settings at any time.

Quick phrases

Translated phrases for emergencies, daily life, medical visits, shopping, and the office — ready in one tap.

Learn sign language

Short video lessons cover core Ugandan Sign Language, starting with "Hello," "Help," and "Thank you."

Guided onboarding

A five-page walkthrough explains SOS, profile setup, language, and phrases before first use.

Built-in accessibility

Vibration and visual cues confirm every action, with high-contrast text that works with screen readers.

How it works

From tap to sent message, in six steps.

This is the exact sequence the app runs the moment the SOS button is pressed.

Check the emergency contact

The app confirms a contact is on file. If one is missing, SOS stays disabled until it's added.

Build the message

An emergency message is assembled with the user's current location attached.

Open the default SMS app

SilentHelp hands off to the phone's own messaging app using url_launcher — no new accounts, no separate inbox.

Log the attempt as prepared

The moment the message is built, it's recorded locally with a timestamp.

Log the send

Once the SMS app opens, the status updates to opened_in_sms.

Confirm on-screen

A toast and haptic pulse confirm the alert is on its way, without relying on sound.

Technical implementation

A straightforward Flutter build, kept local-first.

No backend dependency for core safety features — the emergency flow works from data already on the device.

Framework
Flutter / Dart — Android & iOS from one codebase
State
Riverpod providers for profile, emergency logic, and logs
Navigation
Go Router with a ShellRoute bottom-tab shell
Localization
Easy Localization — English, Swahili, Luganda
Location
Geolocator + Geocoding for SOS coordinates
Storage
Shared Preferences for profile, logs, and settings
Media
Video Player for sign language demos
Messaging
URL Launcher to hand off to the SMS app
Min target
Android API 21+
  • ShellRoute navigation keeps Emergency, Talk, Phrases, ID, Learn, and Settings one tab away at all times.
  • Onboarding sits outside the main shell, gating first-time use until the walkthrough is complete.
  • Provider-based state keeps profile completeness, emergency logic, and history in sync across screens.
  • Everything persists locally — profile, onboarding status, language choice, and SOS history all live in Shared Preferences.
